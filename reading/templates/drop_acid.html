Day 169: Drop Acid by Dr. David Perlmutter

Introduction

Drop Acid by neurologist Dr. David Perlmutter introduces uric acid as a powerful but overlooked factor in metabolic and brain health. Published in 2022, the book reveals that elevated uric acid levels—not just fat, carbs, or calories—may be a root cause of obesity, diabetes, hypertension, fatty liver, and cognitive decline. Perlmutter offers a scientific yet accessible guide to understanding, measuring, and lowering uric acid to improve long-term health.

Summary

  • Uric acid is more than gout: High levels can cause fat storage, insulin resistance, and inflammation.
  • Main culprits: Fructose, alcohol, purine-rich meats, dehydration, and poor kidney function.
  • How it harms: Disrupts mitochondrial energy, increases oxidative stress, and promotes brain aging.
  • Key framework: The LUV protocol – Lower intake, Urine test regularly, and Value uric acid as a health marker.
  • Includes: Food lists, supplement recommendations, recipes, and self-testing strategies.

Core Concept

The core message of Drop Acid is: Uric acid is a metabolic switch that contributes to obesity, chronic disease, and brain decline. Controlling it may be the key to reversing the modern health crisis.

By keeping uric acid below 5.5 mg/dL, you can improve energy, cognition, and metabolic function.

Key Insights and Strategies

  • Fructose is uniquely harmful: It drives uric acid production and liver fat accumulation.
  • Uric acid precedes disease: Levels rise years before diabetes or hypertension appear.
  • Evolutionary context: Our ancestors benefited from uric acid’s fat-storing effects; now it backfires.
  • Measure and monitor: Uric acid can be tracked via home tests or simple bloodwork.
  • Natural solutions: Vitamin C, quercetin, luteolin, hydration, and smart food swaps can help.

Quotes to Remember

“Uric acid is more than a gout issue—it's a central player in metabolic dysfunction.”
“We’ve been so focused on fat and sugar that we’ve missed a powerful trigger hiding in plain sight.”
“Lowering uric acid may be the missing link in reversing metabolic disease.”

Practical Application

  • Eliminate high-fructose foods: Cut sugary drinks, fruit juice, and processed foods with corn syrup.
  • Limit purines: Reduce organ meats, beer, and excessive red meat intake.
  • Hydrate consistently: Support kidney function with plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Track uric acid: Use a uric acid blood monitor or ask for bloodwork; aim for < 5.5 mg/dL.
  • Use supportive supplements: Quercetin and vitamin C may naturally lower uric acid when taken properly.

Interesting Facts and Stories

  • Uric acid was once lifesaving: It helped ancient humans store fat during times of famine.
  • Even “healthy” foods can spike it: Honey, agave, and dried fruits contain high fructose levels.
  • Brain effects are real: High uric acid is linked to fatigue, mental fog, and dementia risk.
  • Perlmutter practices what he preaches: After testing his own uric acid, he made changes and saw health improvements.
  • Drop Acid may redefine metabolic health: It introduces uric acid as a critical but underutilized biomarker.
