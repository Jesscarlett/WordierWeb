Day 156: Fast. Feast. Repeat. by Gin Stephens

Introduction

In a world saturated with diets, food rules, and willpower-driven restrictions, Fast. Feast. Repeat. offers a refreshing approach: intermittent fasting isn’t about what you eat—it’s about when. Written by teacher-turned-health-coach Gin Stephens, this book makes the science and practice of intermittent fasting (IF) accessible, flexible, and empowering.

Published in 2020, Fast. Feast. Repeat. blends personal experience, community anecdotes, and research to promote a sustainable lifestyle rather than a crash diet. It’s particularly popular among women seeking weight loss, metabolic health, and food freedom—without counting calories.

Summary

The book is structured into three key sections:

  • Fast: Introduces the science and history of IF, insulin regulation, autophagy, and metabolic flexibility.
  • Feast: Promotes mindful, satisfying eating with food freedom rather than restriction.
  • Repeat: Helps readers create sustainable routines with methods like 16:8, 18:6, OMAD, and alternate day fasting.

Stephens guides readers through customization, plateaus, social settings, and long-term mindset shifts.

Core Concept

The core message of Fast. Feast. Repeat. is: Intermittent fasting isn’t a diet—it’s a sustainable rhythm that aligns with your body’s natural systems.

It helps lower insulin, trigger fat burning, and support cellular repair, all without obsessing over food or numbers.

Key Insights and Strategies

  • Clean fasting is essential: Only plain water, black coffee, unflavored tea, and mineral water during the fast.
  • Fasting lowers insulin: Allowing the body to tap into fat stores for energy.
  • Adaptation takes time: The first 28 days are for adjusting your metabolism and hunger hormones.
  • Eating windows bring freedom: Ditch calorie counting and eat until naturally satisfied.
  • Autophagy repairs cells: Longer fasts activate cellular cleanup for longevity and detoxification.

Quotes to Remember

“Fasting is not about starving yourself. It’s about tapping into your body’s natural ability to thrive.”
“Hunger is not an emergency.”
“Fast clean. Feast deliciously. Repeat forever.”

Practical Application

  • Start with 16:8: Fast for 16 hours, eat within an 8-hour window. Adjust as needed.
  • Drink water often: Stay hydrated and support detox and appetite regulation.
  • Avoid artificial sweeteners: They can spike insulin and increase hunger even without calories.
  • Eat until satisfied: Focus on nutrient-dense foods without obsessing over macros or calories.
  • Track non-scale victories: Notice improvements in energy, mood, skin, digestion, and sleep.

Interesting Facts and Stories

  • Stephens lost over 80 pounds with IF: Her journey from dieting frustration to sustainable health led her to write and coach others.
  • Fasting has evolutionary roots: Humans evolved in feast-famine cycles; constant eating is unnatural for metabolism.
  • “Clean fast” breakthrough: Many experience stalled results until removing flavors and sweeteners during fasts.
  • Improved brain function: IF enhances focus, energy, and mood for many through stable blood sugar and ketone production.
  • Large IF community support: Real-world results from thousands in her online groups reinforce the book’s methods.
