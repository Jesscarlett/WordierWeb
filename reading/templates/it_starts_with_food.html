Day 163: It Starts With Food by Dallas & Melissa Hartwig

Introduction

Before The Whole30 became a movement, there was It Starts With Food—the foundational book where certified sports nutritionists Dallas and Melissa Hartwig introduced their science-backed elimination and reintroduction program to the world. Published in 2012, this book is more than a diet guide—it’s a deep dive into how food affects your hormones, metabolism, brain, gut, immune system, and long-term health.

With a no-nonsense tone and actionable steps, the Hartwigs explain how eliminating certain foods for 30 days can radically transform your health, habits, and mindset.

Summary

  • The Four Goals of Food: Support healthy psychological and hormonal responses, a healthy gut, and reduce inflammation.
  • Foods to eliminate: Sugar, grains, legumes, dairy, alcohol, and all processed items.
  • Foods to eat: Animal protein, vegetables, fruits, healthy fats, herbs, and spices.
  • After 30 days: Gradually reintroduce eliminated foods to see how they affect your health and mood.
  • Biological & psychological effects: The book explains how hyperpalatable foods impact hormones, cravings, and behavior.

Core Concept

The core message of It Starts With Food is: Food is the foundation of health, and by changing what you eat for 30 days, you can change everything—from digestion and cravings to inflammation, mood, and energy.

Eliminating harmful foods and replacing them with healing whole foods gives the body a much-needed reset.

Key Insights and Strategies

  • Modern foods override biology: Highly processed combos of fat, sugar, and salt hijack the brain’s reward system.
  • Gut health drives whole-body health: Grains and dairy can cause inflammation and leaky gut.
  • Control blood sugar swings: Stable insulin is key to energy and fat regulation.
  • The gut-brain axis is powerful: What you eat influences hormones, neurotransmitters, and behavior.
  • Elimination is necessary: The only way to uncover food sensitivities is to remove and reintroduce them.

Quotes to Remember

“The food you eat either makes you more healthy or less healthy. Those are your options.”
“This is not hard. Don’t you dare tell us this is hard.”
“You can’t just eat healthy once in a while and expect to be healthy.”

Practical Application

  • Do the 30-day reset: Follow the Whole30-style elimination plan using only real, whole foods.
  • Skip the scale: Focus on non-scale victories like energy, sleep, and mood.
  • Prepare in advance: Batch cook, plan meals, and stock compliant pantry staples.
  • Label reading is essential: Look out for hidden sugars, soy, and preservatives.
  • Reintroduce slowly: One food group at a time—observe how your body responds.

Interesting Facts and Stories

  • This book started the Whole30: It introduced the original reset program that evolved into a global wellness brand.
  • Psychology meets physiology: The Hartwigs highlight how food affects emotional health and decision-making.
  • Beyond paleo: Though it shares paleo principles, the program is a structured, short-term reset with clear guidelines.
  • Science-backed but accessible: Combines clear biology lessons with real-life testimonials and tough love.
  • Transformational results: Readers report reversal of autoimmune symptoms, fatigue, migraines, and more.
