Day 17: Make Your Bed by Admiral William H. McRaven

Introduction

Make Your Bed: Little Things That Can Change Your Life... And Maybe the World by Admiral William H. McRaven is a motivational book that draws on the author’s experiences in Navy SEAL training and military service to offer life lessons that can lead to personal growth, success, and positive change. The book is inspired by Admiral McRaven’s commencement address at the University of Texas, which went viral and reached millions of people around the world. Through his ten key principles learned from SEAL training, McRaven encourages readers to tackle challenges, overcome adversity, and contribute to a greater good, all starting with small, everyday actions like making your bed.

Summary

Make Your Bed is divided into ten chapters, each reflecting one of McRaven's key life lessons learned through his military training and service. The principles discussed in the book focus on perseverance, discipline, teamwork, and resilience.

Chapter One: Start Your Day with a Task Completed

McRaven begins by discussing the importance of making your bed every morning. He explains that making your bed is a simple task that sets the tone for the rest of your day, creating a sense of accomplishment. It’s a small action that can help you build discipline and routine. McRaven reflects on his time in SEAL training, where the recruits were required to make their beds perfectly every day. Even in the midst of challenging, exhausting training, the simple act of making your bed gave a sense of pride and control, and it showed the importance of attention to detail.

Chapter Two: You Can't Go It Alone

In this chapter, McRaven highlights the importance of teamwork and supporting one another through difficult times. He shares stories from SEAL training, emphasizing how working together as a team can help you overcome seemingly impossible challenges. Whether in military missions or in everyday life, success often depends on the support and collaboration of others. McRaven teaches that relying on your teammates, offering support, and knowing when to ask for help are critical in achieving success.

Chapter Three: Only the Size of Your Heart Matters

McRaven discusses how size, strength, or physical ability are not the key factors in success; it’s the size of your heart—the determination, grit, and willingness to push through adversity—that truly matters. He uses examples from SEAL training, where recruits who may not have been the strongest physically, but demonstrated resilience and tenacity, were able to excel and complete their training. The message here is that mental toughness, perseverance, and heart are more important than physical abilities.

Chapter Four: Get Over Being a Sugar Cookie

In this chapter, McRaven shares the story of a Navy SEAL training exercise where recruits were called "sugar cookies," meaning they were singled out to perform extra, humiliating tasks for not meeting standards. The lesson McRaven emphasizes is that life isn’t fair, and sometimes you will be unfairly treated or face setbacks. The key to overcoming this is not to quit, but to keep moving forward despite the adversity. You need to embrace the challenges, learn from them, and continue to persevere.

Chapter Five: Don't Fear the Circus

McRaven explains that in Navy SEAL training, the "circus" was an additional set of physically demanding tasks designed to push recruits to their limits. Rather than being intimidated by these difficult challenges, McRaven encourages embracing them, as they test your limits and help build strength and resilience. The lesson here is that when faced with tough challenges, you should push through them, knowing they will help you grow and become stronger.

Chapter Six: Slide Down the Obstacle Head First

McRaven encourages taking risks and approaching challenges with confidence, even when the outcome is uncertain. He shares a story of a particularly difficult obstacle in SEAL training, where recruits were encouraged to slide down a steep incline head first, despite the potential for injury. The lesson is that sometimes, you need to take risks and step outside your comfort zone in order to achieve success. Facing fear head-on can lead to growth and accomplishment.

Chapter Seven: Don't Back Down from the Sharks

McRaven recounts a time when he had to face the terrifying challenge of swimming with sharks during SEAL training. He explains that fear is a natural part of life, but instead of backing down, we must confront our fears and push through them. The message is that we often encounter "sharks" in life—fears or obstacles that seem intimidating—but the only way to overcome them is to face them with courage and determination.

Chapter Eight: Be Your Very Best in Your Darkest Moments

In this chapter, McRaven reflects on how true character is revealed in the toughest moments. He discusses how, during SEAL missions and in combat, the best leaders and teammates are the ones who rise to the occasion when things seem bleak. The lesson is that when facing your darkest moments, you must dig deep and give your best effort, because that’s when your true potential is revealed.

Chapter Nine: Rise to the Occasion

McRaven talks about the importance of stepping up when others are relying on you. He recalls moments during SEAL missions and training when recruits had to step up and take responsibility, even in high-pressure situations. The lesson is that you should be prepared to rise to the occasion when needed, knowing that others are depending on you. This requires mental preparedness, confidence, and the ability to stay focused during stressful situations.

Chapter Ten: Give People Hope

In the final chapter, McRaven reflects on the importance of giving others hope and inspiration. He shares how, during SEAL training, some recruits were ready to quit, but when their teammates supported them and gave them hope, they were able to keep going. McRaven encourages readers to lead by example and offer hope to others, especially during tough times. The ability to inspire and uplift others is a key part of leadership and success.

Story

One of the most powerful stories McRaven shares is about a fellow SEAL who was unable to swim, yet through sheer determination and support from his teammates, he was able to finish SEAL training. Despite being called a "sugar cookie" and having numerous challenges, he did not give up. McRaven emphasizes how the support of others and the ability to keep pushing forward in the face of adversity are key to achieving success.

Key Study Points

  • Start Your Day with a Task Completed: Begin each day with a simple task, like making your bed, to create a sense of accomplishment and start your day with purpose.
  • Teamwork and Support: Success often depends on the collaboration and support of others. Lean on your teammates and offer help when needed.
  • Mental Toughness Over Physical Strength: The size of your heart and your resilience is more important than physical abilities in achieving success.
  • Embrace Challenges: Life will often throw challenges your way, but the key to overcoming them is to face them head-on, even when they seem daunting.
  • Lead by Example: In times of crisis, the best leaders rise to the occasion. Be the one others can rely on during tough times.

Key Concepts and Explanation

  • Mental Resilience: McRaven emphasizes that mental toughness is key to overcoming challenges. It’s about pushing through discomfort, embracing difficulty, and maintaining focus even in the face of adversity.
  • Discipline and Responsibility: Through examples from SEAL training, McRaven illustrates how discipline and personal responsibility are essential for success. Every action, no matter how small, contributes to the bigger picture.
  • Leadership and Hope: One of the greatest traits of a leader is the ability to give hope to others. Whether in the military or in everyday life, showing others that they can persevere can help them achieve their goals.

Quotes

  • "If you want to change the world, start by making your bed."
  • "You must be your very best in your darkest moments."
  • "It’s the little things that are the most important, and when you are doing them right, they can have a huge impact."
  • "The greatest honor of all is to be able to serve those around you."

Fun or Surprising Fact

Admiral McRaven’s commencement address at the University of Texas, which served as the inspiration for *Make Your Bed*, went viral and was viewed by millions of people around the world. It became one of the most-watched commencement speeches in history.

Real-Life Application

The principles in *Make Your Bed* can be applied in many areas of life, from career to personal development. By starting your day with small accomplishments, embracing challenges, working well with others, and staying resilient in difficult times, you can overcome obstacles and achieve success. McRaven’s emphasis on leadership, discipline, and hope makes this book a powerful guide for anyone looking to improve their life.

Recommended Books

  • The Obstacle Is the Way by Ryan Holiday – A book on using adversity as a path to personal growth and success.
  • Extreme Ownership by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin – A book about leadership and accountability based on military principles.
