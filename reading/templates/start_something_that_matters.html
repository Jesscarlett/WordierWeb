Day 40: Start Something That Matters by Blake Mycoskie

Introduction

Start Something That Matters by Blake Mycoskie is an inspiring call to action for aspiring entrepreneurs and changemakers. Published in 2011, the book chronicles Mycoskie’s journey as the founder of TOMS, a socially conscious company that pioneered the One-for-One giving model. The book combines practical advice with compelling stories to demonstrate how individuals can create successful businesses while making a positive impact on the world. At its core, Start Something That Matters is about aligning passion and purpose to build something meaningful, whether it’s a company, a project, or a movement.

Summary

Mycoskie structures the book around six principles that guide readers in starting something that matters:

1. Find Your Story
Mycoskie emphasizes the power of storytelling in creating a brand that resonates. Sharing an authentic narrative—like the inspiration behind TOMS’ mission to provide shoes to children in need—can help build trust and emotional connections with customers.

2. Face Your Fears
Fear of failure often holds people back from pursuing their ideas. Mycoskie encourages readers to embrace vulnerability, take risks, and view challenges as opportunities for growth.

3. Be Resourceful Without Resources
Mycoskie shares stories of how TOMS started with limited resources, emphasizing that creativity and determination are more important than capital. He encourages aspiring entrepreneurs to leverage what they have and focus on solving real problems.

4. Keep It Simple
Simplicity is key to both business models and messaging. Mycoskie highlights how TOMS’ straightforward One-for-One model helped the company gain traction and inspire customers.

5. Build Trust
Trust is essential for building strong relationships with customers, partners, and employees. Mycoskie advises leaders to prioritize transparency, authenticity, and accountability in all aspects of their work.

6. Give Something Back
Mycoskie underscores the importance of integrating giving into the DNA of a business. By aligning profit with purpose, companies can create lasting impact while inspiring others to join their mission.

Story

A pivotal story in the book recounts Mycoskie’s trip to Argentina, where he witnessed children suffering from a lack of proper footwear. This experience inspired the creation of TOMS and its One-for-One model, where every pair of shoes sold provides a pair to a child in need. The simplicity and emotional resonance of this story became a cornerstone of TOMS’ brand and success.

Key Study Points

1. Harness the Power of Storytelling: Share an authentic and compelling narrative to build connections.
2. Embrace Fear: Use challenges and setbacks as opportunities for growth and learning.
3. Leverage Creativity: Focus on solving problems with limited resources.
4. Simplify Your Approach: Make your business model and messaging easy to understand.
5. Cultivate Trust: Foster transparency and authenticity in all relationships.
6. Incorporate Giving: Align profit with purpose to create meaningful impact.

Key Concepts and Explanation

One-for-One Model
This simple yet revolutionary model integrates giving directly into the business strategy, ensuring that every product sold contributes to a greater cause.

Storytelling as a Branding Tool
Mycoskie highlights how sharing an authentic story can create emotional resonance and differentiate a brand in a crowded marketplace.

The Power of Purpose
Mycoskie argues that businesses with a clear mission and values attract loyal customers, motivated employees, and meaningful partnerships.

Quotes

“The most significant opportunities will be found in times of greatest difficulty.”
“If you want to make a difference, you have to start something that matters.”
“Inspiration is like a flame—it has to be fed to keep burning.”

Fun or Surprising Fact

TOMS was one of the first companies to integrate a giving model directly into its business operations, inspiring a wave of social enterprises globally.

Real-Life Application

Entrepreneurship: Apply the One-for-One model or create a mission-driven approach for your business.
Leadership: Use storytelling to inspire and motivate your team.
Innovation: Focus on solving real problems with creative and resourceful solutions.
Social Impact: Align your work with a purpose that creates positive change in the world.

Recommended Books

The Purpose Economy by Aaron Hurst: Examines how purpose-driven work is reshaping careers, organizations, and markets.
