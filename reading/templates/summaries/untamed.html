Day 271: Untamed by Glennon Doyle

Introduction

Untamed by Glennon Doyle, published in 2020, is a powerful memoir and self-help manifesto about reclaiming one’s identity, trusting inner knowing, and breaking free from societal expectations. Blending storytelling, reflection, and insight, Doyle urges readers—especially women—to stop living according to the roles they’ve been handed and start living according to their deepest truths.

The book began as Doyle’s story of walking away from a life built on external approval—marriage, religion, motherhood, and perfectionism—and choosing instead to build an honest life rooted in authenticity, intuition, and courage. But it expands into a broader call: Stop being tamed. Become who you were meant to be.

The Cheetah and the Cage

Doyle opens with her famous “cheetah story.” At a zoo, she watches a cheetah trained to act like a domestic dog. It ignites a revelation: women, too, are raised to be obedient, accommodating, and “good,” even when their nature tells them they are meant for wildness, freedom, and power.

The question becomes: What would I do if I trusted myself completely?

This question fuels the book.

The Moment of Awakening

Doyle describes a turning point during a conference when she sees Abby Wambach, the retired soccer star, walk across the room—and instantly knows she’s in love. At the time, Doyle is a married Christian mother, famous for advocating for traditional marriage.

This moment forces her to confront a painful truth: the life she is living no longer fits.

She chooses honesty, ending her marriage and stepping into a new partnership with Abby. This decision becomes the book’s core metaphor: We lose ourselves by trying to be “good.” We find ourselves by being true.

The Voice of Knowing

Doyle introduces the concept of the “inner Knowing”—a quiet voice beneath fear and conditioning. This voice guides us toward what is right for us, even when it disrupts expectations.

She encourages practices of silence, reflection, and self-trust:

  • Sit with yourself.
  • Listen without judgment.
  • Believe your own feelings.
  • Choose your own path.

This principle underpins the journey of becoming untamed.

Undoing Conditioning

Doyle breaks down societal messages imposed on women:

  • Be small.
  • Be selfless.
  • Be pleasing.
  • Be calm.
  • Be grateful, even when you're unhappy.

She argues these messages produce obedient women, not joyful or powerful ones. To become “untamed,” women must unlearn these expectations and reclaim agency.

Doyle writes: “The braver we are, the luckier we get.”

Love, Motherhood, and Boundaries

Doyle reframes motherhood not as martyrdom but as modeling freedom. Children, she writes, don’t need a self-sacrificing mother—they need a fully alive one.

She advocates for:

  • Emotional honesty
  • Healthy boundaries
  • Letting children feel discomfort
  • Seeing kids as their own selves, not extensions of parents

Her relationship with Abby becomes an example of building partnerships rooted in truth, equality, and mutual growth.

Pain as a Compass

Doyle insists that we must feel our feelings—not numb or avoid them. She explains how pain, instead of something to escape, can be a teacher pointing toward necessary change.

Her message:

  • Pain is information.
  • Numbing leads to disconnection.
  • Feeling leads to clarity, compassion, and transformation.

Racism, Activism, and Social Responsibility

Doyle widens the book’s scope beyond personal transformation. She addresses:

  • Anti-racism
  • LGBTQ+ advocacy
  • Body autonomy
  • Religious reform
  • Gender roles

She argues that becoming untamed isn’t just personal—it’s political. When individuals free themselves from conditioning, they also gain the clarity and courage to confront and change unjust systems.

Becoming Untamed

The book ends with a manifesto of empowerment:

  • Stop abandoning yourself.
  • Trust your intuition.
  • Ask for more.
  • Let go of “good” to become free.
  • Your truest life is on the other side of discomfort.
  • You can build a life that feels like your own.

Doyle’s final message is simple: You were born wild. You were taught to be tame. You can choose to return to yourself.

Why This Book Matters

Untamed became a cultural phenomenon because it speaks directly to the struggle between external expectation and internal truth. Doyle’s storytelling is raw, vulnerable, and galvanizing.

The book resonates with readers seeking:

  • Authenticity
  • Healing
  • Personal power
  • Freedom from conditioning
  • Courage to disrupt the status quo

It’s a roadmap for anyone who feels stuck inside a life they didn’t fully choose.

Key Takeaways and Insights

  • Society conditions women to be small, agreeable, and self-sacrificing—but this is unnatural and harmful.
  • True freedom comes from trusting one’s inner voice, not external approval.
  • Love, parenting, and community flourish when rooted in authenticity and boundaries.
  • Pain is a guide and must be felt to be understood.
  • Becoming untamed is both a personal and political act—it changes the self and society.
  • Happiness and fulfillment require honesty, courage, and self-respect.
  • You alone are responsible for building a life that feels true.
