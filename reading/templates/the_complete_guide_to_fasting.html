Day 158: The Complete Guide to Fasting by Dr. Jason Fung (with Jimmy Moore)

Introduction

Fasting is often misunderstood as starvation or extreme deprivation, but in The Complete Guide to Fasting, Canadian nephrologist Dr. Jason Fung reframes it as a powerful, flexible tool for healing the body. Co-written with author and podcaster Jimmy Moore, this 2016 book provides a comprehensive roadmap for using intermittent and extended fasting to reverse insulin resistance, lose weight, and restore metabolic health.

Drawing on clinical experience treating type 2 diabetes and obesity, Fung demystifies the science of fasting, busts myths, and offers detailed guidance for safe, effective fasting at any level.

Summary

  • Fasting is not starvation: It’s a deliberate, controlled break from eating for therapeutic benefit.
  • Humans are built to fast: Our ancestors regularly alternated between feasting and fasting.
  • Fasting works by lowering insulin: This triggers fat burning, growth hormone, and autophagy.
  • Fasting options: From daily intermittent fasting (16:8, OMAD) to extended 24–72 hour fasts.
  • Supportive strategies: Electrolytes, clean fluids, managing hunger, and refeeding protocols.

Core Concept

The core message of The Complete Guide to Fasting is: Fasting is a safe, natural, and highly effective way to restore hormonal balance and metabolic health—especially when insulin resistance is the root cause.

Used strategically, fasting can promote fat loss, stabilize blood sugar, and reverse chronic diseases.

Key Insights and Strategies

  • Fasting lowers insulin better than dieting: It gives the body a true hormonal reset.
  • Fasting does not crash metabolism: In fact, short-term fasts increase energy and fat burning hormones.
  • Our bodies evolved to fast: It's how we survived periods of scarcity—without harm.
  • Longer fasts offer deeper healing: Promote autophagy, reduce inflammation, improve insulin sensitivity.
  • Hydration and electrolytes matter: Sodium, magnesium, and potassium help reduce common side effects.

Quotes to Remember

“Fasting is the oldest dietary intervention in the world, yet we’ve forgotten its power.”
“When you fast, your body doesn’t shut down—it switches fuel sources.”
“Don’t eat less. Eat less often.”

Practical Application

  • Start small: Try 16:8 or a 24-hour fast once or twice weekly.
  • Drink water, tea, or coffee: No calories or sweeteners during the fast.
  • Eat whole foods during meals: Focus on nutrient-dense, low-carb meals for insulin control.
  • Supplement electrolytes: Bone broth, sea salt, or magnesium to avoid fatigue and cramps.
  • Consult your doctor: Especially if you take medication or manage chronic illness.

Interesting Facts and Stories

  • Fasting reverses type 2 diabetes: Many of Dr. Fung’s patients came off insulin entirely using fasting protocols.
  • Extended fasts yield benefits beyond weight loss: Users report clearer skin, improved mood, and increased focus.
  • Fasting is easier than dieting: Once fat-adapted, hunger diminishes and no calorie counting is required.
  • “Starvation mode” is misunderstood: The body maintains muscle and energy during fasts of several days.
  • Women can fast too: Includes guidance for menstrual cycles, menopause, and hormone balance.
