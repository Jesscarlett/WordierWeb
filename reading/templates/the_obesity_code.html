Day 157: The Obesity Code by Dr. Jason Fung

Introduction

What if obesity isn’t just about calories in versus calories out? In The Obesity Code, Canadian nephrologist and fasting expert Dr. Jason Fung argues that insulin—not calories—is the key driver of weight gain. Backed by extensive clinical experience and scientific research, the book challenges conventional wisdom about dieting, exercise, and fat loss.

Published in 2016, The Obesity Code has become a cornerstone of the intermittent fasting and low-carb communities. It provides a deep dive into the hormonal drivers of obesity, especially insulin resistance, and outlines practical strategies to lose weight and reverse type 2 diabetes—without starving yourself or relying on willpower.

Summary

  • Obesity is a hormonal disorder: Overeating is a symptom, not the root cause.
  • Insulin controls fat storage: High insulin tells the body to store fat and blocks fat burning.
  • Insulin resistance is driven by constant eating and refined carbs: Snacking and high-carb diets promote weight gain over time.
  • Calorie restriction fails long-term: It slows metabolism and increases hunger.
  • The solution is hormonal balance: Lower insulin via intermittent fasting and whole-food, low-carb meals.

Core Concept

The core message of The Obesity Code is: To lose weight and keep it off, you must address the root cause—insulin resistance—not just calories.

By lowering insulin levels through intermittent fasting and reduced sugar intake, you restore your body’s natural fat-burning capabilities.

Key Insights and Strategies

  • Insulin drives fat storage: More than calories, insulin determines if you store or burn fat.
  • Snacking keeps insulin high: Even small, frequent meals prevent fat burning.
  • Dieting alone doesn't work: It triggers hormonal changes that lead to weight regain.
  • Fasting lowers insulin effectively: Regular fasts improve metabolism, reverse insulin resistance, and promote autophagy.
  • Refined carbs and sugar are the enemy: Remove them to dramatically lower insulin levels.

Quotes to Remember

“Obesity is not a caloric imbalance; it is a hormonal imbalance.”
“The key to weight loss is not reducing calories—it’s reducing insulin.”
“You can’t fix a hormonal problem with a caloric solution.”

Practical Application

  • Start with 16:8 intermittent fasting: Fast for 16 hours, eat in an 8-hour window.
  • Eliminate refined carbs and sugar: Ditch sodas, bread, pasta, and processed snacks.
  • Eat whole, real foods: Focus on vegetables, protein, healthy fats, and leafy greens.
  • Stop snacking: Allow insulin to drop between meals to burn fat.
  • Use longer fasts: 24–36 hour fasts can help break through plateaus and reset insulin sensitivity.

Interesting Facts and Stories

  • Diabetes reversed with fasting: Dr. Fung helped many patients come off insulin entirely through dietary changes and fasting.
  • Obesity rose with low-fat guidelines: The 1970s shift toward low-fat, high-carb diets correlates with modern obesity trends.
  • Insulin overpowers willpower: Hormones, not discipline, dictate fat storage and hunger signals.
  • “Breakfast is the most important meal” is marketing: A slogan invented by cereal companies, not biology.
  • “Biggest Loser” contestants regained weight: Due to long-term metabolic damage from extreme calorie restriction.
