Day 237: The Sixth Wave by James Bradfield Moody & Bianca Nogrady

Introduction

The Sixth Wave: How to Succeed in a Resource-Limited World by James Bradfield Moody and Bianca Nogrady, published in 2010, offers a powerful framework for understanding the cycles of industrial innovation throughout human history.

The authors argue that we are now entering the sixth wave of innovation—an era defined by the need to solve the pressing challenges of resource scarcity, climate change, and environmental degradation.

This wave is about doing more with less—a shift toward sustainability, efficiency, and a mindset that values regeneration over consumption. It is not just a warning—it’s an invitation to embrace opportunity.

The Theory of Innovation Waves

The book draws on the long-wave theory of economic cycles, which proposes that human history progresses in waves of technological and industrial innovation, each lasting roughly 40-60 years.

  • The first wave was powered by the Industrial Revolution.
  • The second wave centered on steam power and railways.
  • The third wave emerged with electricity and heavy engineering.
  • The fourth wave introduced mass production and oil-driven growth.
  • The fifth wave focused on information technology and digital connectivity.

We are now entering the sixth wave—a global movement driven by sustainability.

What Defines the Sixth Wave?

The sixth wave is about addressing the consequences of the previous waves, characterized by:

  • Resource efficiency: Doing more with less.
  • Renewable energy: Shifting to sustainable power sources.
  • Waste reduction: Designing products to minimize waste.
  • Biomimicry: Learning from nature’s efficient designs.
  • Closed-loop systems: Moving toward circular economies where products are reused and recycled.

Innovation in this wave is driven by necessity—addressing limited resources, growing populations, and climate urgency.

Drivers of the Sixth Wave

  • Environmental limits: The planet’s capacity to absorb pollution is being tested.
  • Economic instability: Scarcity is disrupting markets.
  • Population growth: More people, more demand.
  • Consumer awareness: Demand for greener, ethical products is rising.
  • Government regulation: Policies are pushing sustainable solutions.

These forces are accelerators for innovation in the sixth wave.

Opportunities in the Sixth Wave

Industries and business models poised to succeed include:

  • Clean energy technologies: Solar, wind, geothermal.
  • Green building and sustainable design.
  • Water management and recycling.
  • Waste-to-resource industries.
  • Collaborative consumption and sharing economies.

The sixth wave rewards companies that integrate sustainability from the start.

Mindset Shift: From Consumption to Regeneration

The sixth wave requires a shift in thinking—from measuring success by volume to measuring it by value and sustainability. Systems thinking becomes essential, as industries, communities, and ecosystems are deeply interconnected.

Case Studies and Real-World Examples

  • Companies designing fully recyclable products.
  • Cities adopting zero-waste strategies.
  • Businesses applying biomimicry in materials and systems.
  • Renewable energy companies transforming power grids.

Barriers and Challenges

  • Entrenched industries resistant to change.
  • Economic models focused on short-term profits.
  • Limited consumer understanding about sustainability.
  • Need for stronger regulations and leadership.

The authors believe these can be overcome through innovation, collaboration, and shifting cultural priorities.

Key Takeaways and Insights

  • The sixth wave is driven by sustainability and resource efficiency.
  • Global challenges like climate change are powerful innovation drivers.
  • Industries that adapt early will lead the future economy.
  • The shift is from extraction and consumption to regeneration and circularity.
  • Opportunities are abundant in clean energy, waste management, and collaborative economies.
  • Everyone—consumers, businesses, governments—has a role in shaping this wave.

Why This Book Matters

The Sixth Wave is a roadmap for thriving in a resource-limited world. It offers a hopeful, actionable vision for embracing sustainability as the next great driver of human progress.

The authors challenge readers to think differently about success, prioritize long-term impact, and become part of the transformation already underway.

We are standing at the edge of a profound shift. The sixth wave is here. And those who ride it will help build a smarter, more sustainable world.

The time to act is now.
