Day 53: What Got You Here Won't Get You There by Marshall Goldsmith

Introduction

What Got You Here Won't Get You There: How Successful People Become Even More Successful by Marshall Goldsmith is a powerful guide to achieving professional growth and personal transformation. Published in 2007, the book explores the behaviors and habits that can hold successful individuals back from reaching their full potential. Goldsmith, a renowned executive coach, provides actionable advice for overcoming these limiting behaviors and embracing change. With insights drawn from his extensive experience working with top executives, the book is a must-read for anyone seeking to refine their leadership skills and advance their career.

Summary

The book focuses on identifying and addressing "transactional flaws," which are subtle habits that hinder professional success. Goldsmith outlines the following key themes:

1. The Success Delusion
Goldsmith argues that past success can create a false sense of security, leading individuals to overlook behaviors that may impede future growth. He emphasizes the importance of humility and self-awareness in recognizing these flaws.

2. The Twenty Bad Habits
The book identifies 20 common habits that successful people often exhibit, such as:
- **Winning Too Much:** The drive to win in all situations, even when it’s unnecessary.
- **Adding Too Much Value:** Always trying to improve others’ ideas, which can stifle collaboration.
- **Passing Judgment:** Offering opinions and critiques unnecessarily.
- **Failing to Give Proper Recognition:** Overlooking the contributions of others.
Goldsmith provides strategies for breaking free from these habits.

3. Feedback and Feedforward
Goldsmith highlights the value of seeking constructive feedback and "feedforward," a forward-looking approach to improving future behaviors. He encourages leaders to listen actively and act on the input they receive.

4. The Power of Apology
A sincere apology can repair relationships and build trust. Goldsmith outlines how to apologize effectively and take responsibility for one’s actions.

5. Follow-Up and Discipline
Sustained change requires consistent follow-up and self-discipline. Goldsmith provides tools for tracking progress and maintaining accountability.

Story

One of the book’s most memorable anecdotes involves a senior executive who consistently interrupted colleagues during meetings. With Goldsmith’s coaching, the executive learned to actively listen and hold back comments, transforming his relationships and team dynamics. This story underscores the transformative power of addressing even small behavioral changes.

Key Study Points

1. Embrace Feedback: Seek input from others to identify blind spots and areas for improvement.
2. Address Bad Habits: Recognize and replace behaviors that hinder collaboration and growth.
3. Focus on Relationships: Strengthen interpersonal connections by showing empathy, respect, and humility.
4. Commit to Change: Use follow-up and accountability to sustain improvements.
5. Adopt Feedforward: Shift focus to future actions and behaviors rather than dwelling on past mistakes.

Key Concepts and Explanation

The Need for Behavioral Change
Goldsmith emphasizes that professional success often depends on soft skills and interpersonal behaviors rather than technical expertise. Leaders must adapt and evolve to meet new challenges.

The Impact of Subtle Behaviors
Seemingly minor habits, such as interrupting or failing to recognize others, can have significant negative impacts on relationships and organizational culture.

Active Listening
Goldsmith advocates for listening without judgment or interruption to foster trust and collaboration.

Quotes

“What got you here won’t get you there.”
“Successful people become great leaders when they learn to shift the focus from themselves to others.”
“People will do something—including changing their behavior—only if it can be demonstrated that doing so is in their own best interests as defined by their own values.”

Fun or Surprising Fact

Goldsmith’s coaching framework has been used by Fortune 500 executives worldwide, making him one of the most influential thought leaders in leadership development.

Real-Life Application

Leadership Development: Identify and address habits that may hinder your effectiveness as a leader.
Team Collaboration: Build stronger relationships by fostering trust and recognizing contributions.
Personal Growth: Use feedback and feedforward to drive continuous improvement.
Conflict Resolution: Apply the principles of active listening and apology to resolve conflicts and rebuild trust.

Recommended Books

Emotional Intelligence 2.0 by Travis Bradberry and Jean Greaves: Explores how to enhance emotional intelligence for personal and professional success.
Dare to Lead by Brené Brown: Provides insights into cultivating courageous leadership and vulnerability.
